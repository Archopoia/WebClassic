Room VIII · Human Scale

Measured to the body

A classical building is neither crushing nor trivial: its doors, stairs, and columns answer to the moving body. A page keeps the same courtesy — a line the eye can track, text the body can rest within, targets the hand can strike.

  • Reading measure≈ 66 characters
  • Body text18px · arm’s length
  • Line height1.7 · room to breathe
  • Touch / click target≥ 6 modules
  • Doorway to figureadmits the body

Nothing here is sized to impress; everything is sized to be used. The visitor should feel welcomed, never dwarfed — the oldest courtesy of the orders.

Room IX · Unity in Variety

One language, many details

Too much sameness is dull; too much variety is chaos. The classical remedy: a single system, expressed with measured difference — the very principle you just watched the five orders obey.

Structure

Layout is drawn first; the grid is the invisible geometry that carries every part.

Hierarchy is decided before a single colour is chosen.

Proportion

Measured relationships bind the parts into a whole; ratios govern type, spacing, and measure alike.

Nothing stands alone — each element answers to the module.

Ornament

Decoration arrives last, and only to reveal structure — a thin rule, an engraved capital, a rare stroke of bronze.

If it hides the building, it is removed.

Ordo · Proportio · Firmitas

Made deliberately. Nothing is accidental. Everything has a place.
