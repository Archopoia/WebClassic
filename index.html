IV · The Five Orders

A character for every purpose

Vignola ranged the orders from plainest to richest. A page, too, may choose its temperament — then hold to it with discipline.

Tuscan

Reliability. Plain shaft, strong spacing, no excess. For engineering & the state.

Doric

Authority. Bold headings, large proportions, strong horizontal divisions.

Ionic

Refinement. Scroll-volutes, balanced whitespace, elegance without excess.

Corinthian

Richness. Acanthus detail, expressive imagery, ornament used with intent.

Composite

Union. Strength and elegance joined — many systems, one unity.

This treatise is set in the Ionic manner: slender proportion, refined lettering, and quiet whitespace.

X · The Rule of Three

Column · Center · Column

The classical triad, held in balance about a central axis — and preserved, never scattered, when the viewport narrows.

Structure

Layout is drawn first. The grid is the invisible geometry that carries every part.

Hierarchy is decided before a single colour is chosen.

Proportion

Measured relationships bind the parts into a whole. Ratios govern type, spacing, and the reading measure alike.

Nothing stands alone; each element answers to the module.

Ornament

Decoration arrives last, and only to reveal structure — a thin rule, an engraved capital, a rare stroke of bronze.

If it hides the building, it is removed.

Ordo · Proportio · Firmitas

Made deliberately. Nothing is accidental. Everything has a place.
